## 🏢 Operational Performance Questions

These are the day-to-day questions an **Operations Analyst** or **Network Performance Analyst** would be expected to answer.

**On-Time Performance (OTP)**
- What is our system-wide on-time departure and arrival rate this month vs. last month?
- Which routes are chronically underperforming against our OTP target?
- How does on-time performance degrade across the day — and at what hour does it collapse?
- What share of our flights are early, on-time, slightly late, and severely late?
- Which departure banks are most affected by delay propagation?

**Delay Causality**
- What percentage of our total delay minutes is controllable (carrier-caused) vs. uncontrollable (weather, NAS)?
- Which delay cause — carrier, NAS, weather, late aircraft, or security — drives the most total delay minutes across the network?
- Is a single delay cause disproportionately affecting one specific carrier or airport?
- How does the mix of delay causes shift between summer and winter schedules?
- For flights delayed more than 2 hours, what is the most common root cause?

**Delay Propagation (Late Aircraft)**
- What proportion of our delays are classified as late aircraft — meaning we caused our own delays?
- At which hub airports does the late-aircraft cascade effect amplify most severely?
- If we reduced our first-bank morning delays by 20%, what would the downstream OTP improvement look like?

## ✈️ Network & Route Strategy Questions

These belong to **Network Planning**, **Schedule Planning**, and **Revenue Management** roles.

- Which routes have the highest average departure delay AND the highest flight frequency — meaning we're consistently disappointing the most passengers?
- Are there routes where our on-time performance is significantly worse than the industry average — suggesting a scheduling or capacity problem?
- Do short-haul routes (under 500 miles) have proportionally worse OTP than long-haul? What does that imply about minimum block time padding?
- Which origin airports generate the most delay minutes before aircraft even leave the gate?
- Which routes have a buffer period that is systematically insufficient — where actual elapsed time consistently exceeds scheduled block time?
- Are there route pairs where delays at the origin airport predict delays at the destination airport hours later?

---

## 🔧 Maintenance & Technical Operations Questions

These align with **MRO (Maintenance, Repair & Overhaul)** and **Technical Operations** analyst roles — and are rare for candidates to demonstrate.

- What percentage of our total delays are attributable to maintenance issues?
- Which aircraft (by tail number or fleet type) generate the most maintenance-related delays?
- Are maintenance delays clustered on specific routes or airports — suggesting a ground equipment or AOG parts availability problem?
- What is the average duration of a maintenance-caused delay vs. a weather-caused delay?
- Is there a seasonal pattern in maintenance delays — suggesting a weather-induced wear pattern on components?
- Which routes should be prioritized for a predictive maintenance program based on delay frequency and duration?

---

## 🏗️ Ground Operations & Turnaround Questions

These are core to **Ground Operations**, **Station Management**, and **Turnaround Analytics** roles.

- At which airports do our actual turnaround times most frequently exceed scheduled turnaround targets?
- What is the average gap between scheduled and actual turnaround time by airport?
- Is there a correlation between short scheduled turnaround windows and higher departure delay rates?
- At which stations would adding 10 minutes of buffer time have the greatest positive effect on OTP?
- Do specific time-of-day slots (e.g., mid-afternoon) have systematically longer turnaround times — suggesting ground crew capacity constraints?
- Which routes have the tightest scheduled turnarounds, and are those routes the same routes with the worst OTP?

---

## 💰 Financial & Cost Impact Questions

These appeal to **Finance**, **Commercial Analytics**, and **Revenue Management** teams — and show business acumen beyond pure operations.

- What is the estimated total passenger compensation cost generated by delays across the network in a given month?
- Which routes generate the most passenger compensation liability — our highest-cost delay problem spots?
- What is the average cost per delayed flight, and how does that vary by delay severity (15–59 min vs. 60–119 min vs. 120+ min)?
- If we eliminated our top 10 worst-performing routes' delays, how much compensation cost would we avoid annually?
- What is the financial ROI of adding an extra ground handler at a hub airport if it reduces turnaround overruns by 15 minutes?
- Is there a threshold delay length (e.g., 90 minutes) beyond which compensation costs increase nonlinearly?

---

## 📊 Benchmarking & Competitive Intelligence Questions

These map to **Competitive Intelligence**, **Strategy**, and **Commercial Analytics** roles.

- How does our system-wide OTP compare to the BTS industry average for the same reporting period?
- On routes where we compete head-to-head with another carrier, who has better on-time performance — and by how much?
- Which carriers are outperforming us on our busiest routes, and is the gap driven by scheduling differences or operational execution?
- On overlapping routes, is a competitor's superior OTP correlated with different scheduled block times or different departure time windows?
- Are there routes where we lead our competitor on OTP despite similar block times — and what explains that advantage?

---

## 📅 Scheduling & Buffer Optimization Questions

These are relevant to **Schedule Planning** and **Operations Research** roles.

- Which routes have buffer times that are consistently consumed — meaning block time padding is structurally insufficient?
- Which routes have excess buffer — where we are padding schedules unnecessarily and reducing asset utilization?
- At what time of day should buffer be concentrated to have the greatest OTP improvement for the least schedule efficiency cost?
- If we added 5 minutes of buffer to our 10 chronically delayed routes, what is the projected OTP improvement?
- Is there a relationship between time of day of departure and buffer adequacy — do afternoon flights need more buffer than morning flights?

---

## 🔮 Predictive & Scenario Analysis Questions

These are more advanced and demonstrate **data science** thinking — high-signal for DS and senior DA roles.

- Based on historical patterns, which flights scheduled for tomorrow are most likely to be delayed?
- Given a weather event forecast at a hub airport, how many downstream flights can we expect to be impacted?
- If we increased ground staff efficiency by 20% at our top 3 delay-prone airports, what is the projected system-wide OTP lift?
- What is the probability that a flight departing after 3PM on a Friday from a given hub will experience a delay greater than 30 minutes?
- Which combination of route, carrier, and departure time is the single highest-risk profile for a severe (120+ min) delay?

## 🎯 How to Use This List in an Interview

| Situation | What to do |
|---|---|
| *"Tell me about a project"* | Pick one question from each section and explain how your analysis answers it |
| *"What KPIs would you track?"* | Pull from **OTP** and **Financial** sections — show you connect operations to cost |
| *"Have you worked with operations data?"* | Use the **Turnaround** and **Maintenance** questions — most candidates skip these |
| *"How would you prioritize improvement efforts?"* | Combine the **Scenario Analysis** and **Cost Impact** questions into a prioritization framework |
| *"What's your business impact?"* | Lead with the **Financial** section — convert delay minutes into dollars |
